I wish...

    My dad's words hit me like a truck. "Grandpa is in the ICU now." Frozen with fear, I felt helpless not being able to do anything to help my grandfather in his fragile health. When my grandpa fell to the floor and lost consciousness after hitting his head, he was rushed to the hospital. During his time there, he had to undergo multiple high-dose computed tomography (CT) scans, which caused significant damage to his body. This experience made me question whether there was a way to reduce the harm of CT scans without sacrificing image quality.
    Four years ago, as a 12-year-old, I was determined to find a solution to this problem. Over the years, I used my growing knowledge of math and logical thinking to try and come up with a solution. Nevertheless, I continued on my quest although there seems no answer in sight.
    After obtaining the qualification of USACO Platinum, I decided that it is time to continue from where I have stopped. I began to navigate both online and offline, consulting people.          
    Although it was hard, I was able to start my project. You have to know, at the time, I only had a basic knowledge of machine learning through some online courses. So it was quite difficult for me, I  faced many challenges when building my program to perform multiple functions. For instance, the Sharp U-net only produces the same image size as the input size, so I was quite bothered when performing super-resolution (increasing the image size). In the end, I decided to combine it with interpolation, making the neural network function as a filter. By using the open-source code and my own creativity, I was able to put all the pieces together and create a program that had the potential to reduce the harm of CT scans.
    Looking back, I am proud of what I have accomplished and how my emotional connection to this problem spurred me to embark on an intellectual journey that led to a breakthrough solution. By turning my personal experience into a scientific inquiry, I was able to create something that had the potential to improve the quality of life for many people. I am excited to continue exploring the world of science and technology in the future and to see where my curiosity and passion will take me next. I know that I will face many challenges and obstacles along the way, but I am determined to continue creating my own path.
